Site Notes

  • There is no "wall" where students hit a lesson, and then give up. The slope pattern is exactly what we were hoping for.
  • Students tend to hit the trial questions first to "check out" what they need to know. Perhaps in the future each lesson will show the exact problem so they can keep in mind what they're trying to solve. Like, who wouldn't look at the test first if given the option? (actual logins could help A/B test something like this)
  • Devices make sense, though the high number of macs (and iPads?) is nice. This could make system setup easier if fewer students need to borrow macs in the future. Also, I wish I could see who added this to discord.
  • While student are learning, they do not at all have a linear path, they will go all over the place, referencing pretty much every lesson all the time. I think a mini viewport with references to previous sessions would be a really cool idea to keep students focused on the current lesson, and use up more screen (it's a little bare by default)
  • there are some theoretical patterns, like a student who day 1 looked at the intro, day 2 lesson 1, and then nothing until a week later when they hit the application question page. Did they realize they needed to know a lot, but didn't like the resource we made, found something else to learn JS, and then come back and take the exam?
  • I wish I could tie students to the actual submission date and entry to see how patterns applied to actual end results. Next round, we should have a sign-in so we can actually tie submissions to journeys.
